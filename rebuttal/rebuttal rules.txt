Dear Dr. Ruogu Chen,

  On behalf of the ICCAD Technical Program leadership team, I am writing
to inform you that the ICCAD 2026 author rebuttal phase has now started.
Please review the guidelines below carefully.

1. Rebuttal Period:

   The rebuttal phase started on June 10, 2026 at 11:59 PM AoE.

   The original rebuttal deadline was June 17, 2026 at 11:59 PM AoE.
However, due to the record number of submissions this year, several TPC
members have been managing exceptionally heavy review loads, and some
papers are still missing one or more reviews. We are actively following
up with TPC members to ensure that the remaining reviews are uploaded by
the end of this week.

   To give authors sufficient time to review all available comments and
prepare meaningful responses, we are extending the rebuttal deadline to
June 20, 2026 at 11:59 PM AoE.

2. Review Fields Visible to Authors:

   During the rebuttal phase, authors will have visibility into the
following review form fields:

   1. Paper Summary
   2. Strengths
   3. Weaknesses
   4. Comments to the Authors
   5. Rebuttal Questions

3. How to Submit Your Rebuttal:

   Authors should visit the following page for instructions on how to
respond to the reviewers’ questions:

   https://iccad.com/2026/rebuttal-phase

4. Scope of the Rebuttal:

   The rebuttal is intended to clarify issues raised by the reviewers
and to respond to their questions. Authors are not allowed to modify,
revise, or resubmit the paper** during the rebuttal phase.

   Please use the rebuttal response only to address reviewer concerns,
correct misunderstandings, and provide clarifications where needed.

5. Response Length:

   The rebuttal response must not exceed 2,000 words.

6. Review Status and Professionalism of Comments:

   Please note that some reviews may not yet be final and may not have
been fully reviewed or “sanitized” by the Technical Program Committee.
Paper shepherds will continue to review and sanitize comments as needed.

  We apologize in advance if any wording in the review comments appears
unprofessional or insufficiently polished. We made the decision to share
available reviews with authors early, despite this risk, so that authors
would have as much time as possible to prepare meaningful rebuttal
responses.

  We greatly appreciate your patience and cooperation as we complete
this important stage of the review process. Thank you for your
contributions to ICCAD 2026.

Best regards,
Ismail Bustany
Program Chair, ICCAD 2026

Dear Dr. Ruogu, Chen

I would like to share several important updates regarding the rebuttal
phase:

1. In response to requests from several authors, we have increased the
rebuttal word limit from 2,000 to 7,500 words.

2. TPC members have been instructed not to search paper titles on arXiv
or other public repositories while reviewing submissions. They have also
been instructed that, if a submission is inadvertently found to have
appeared on arXiv, this must not influence the review or decision
process.

3. We are still following up with a small number of delayed reviewers.
We sincerely apologize to authors whose papers have not yet received the
full set of reviews, and we are actively working to resolve this as
quickly as possible.

This is the first time ICCAD is conducting a formal rebuttal process,
and it is a learning experience for all of us. We welcome your feedback
as we work to make the process fair, constructive, and effective.
